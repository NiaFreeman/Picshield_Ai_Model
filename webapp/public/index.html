About PicShield

PicShield helps you check photos locally before you decide to share or upload them. By default the demo analyzes images in your browser (on-device) so no photos are uploaded unless you explicitly choose to do so.

The goal is to give you a quick, privacy-preserving preview: if a photo looks like it contains potentially sensitive information, PicShield will flag it so you can take action (blur, move to Hidden, strip metadata, or delete) before sharing.

How the AI works

Yes — the detection runs on trained machine learning models. The models were trained using the notebooks in this repo and the browser demo performs inference locally using TensorFlow.js. That means the image analysis is done by a model (machine learning) running in your browser; PicShield does not send your photos to a cloud service unless you explicitly consent.

Privacy Center

PicShield analyzes photos locally by default. We will never upload photos or raw model outputs without your explicit consent.

Use the controls below to manage local personalization and telemetry. You can also upload specific photos for optional cloud analysis; this is always per-photo and requires explicit consent.
